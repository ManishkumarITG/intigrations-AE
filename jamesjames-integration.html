James & James Integration — Shopify Edited Order Sync (Account Editor)

3PL Integrations  •  Last updated: April 2026

This guide helps merchants using James & James (3PL/WMS (Third-Party Logistics / Warehouse Management System)) understand how to configure the integration so that Account Editor order edits are always reflected correctly. James & James does not read live edits — order changes must be synced before James & James downloads the order.

⚠️

Important: James & James uses the Account Editor Delayed Master Flow to delay order downloads. Payment is held in Authorized state while customers edit, then the Flow captures payment automatically after the grace period — triggering James & James to download the finalized order.

🔄 How the Integration Works

James & James downloads orders from Shopify only when payment status is “Paid”. The Account Editor Delayed Master Flow creates a grace period between order creation and payment capture — giving you time to edit the order in Account Editor before James & James ever sees it.

1Order Created

Customer completes checkout → Order is created in Shopify.

2Payment Authorized

Payment status remains “Authorized” (not “Paid”). The payment is held but not captured.

3Grace Period Active

The editing window opens (e.g., 30 minutes). Customer can edit the order in Account Editor — add items, remove items, change quantities, apply discount codes, update address.

4James & James Does Not Download

Because payment status is still Authorized, James & James does not see the order. The order is completely invisible to James & James during the entire editing window.

5Grace Period Ends

The editing window expires. No further customer edits are accepted.

6Payment Captured

Account Editor Delayed Master Flow automatically captures the payment. Order status updates to “Paid”.

7James & James Downloads Order

James & James sees the order for the first time with all edits already included — correct items, quantities, discounts, and totals.

🪜 Step-by-Step Setup
📋 Step 1 — Enable Manual Payment Capture in Shopify
✅ Step-by-Step Configuration

Set Shopify to hold payment in Authorized state so James & James cannot import the order until editing is complete and payment is captured.

  1. 1Log in to Shopify Admin
  2. 2Go to Settings → Payments
  3. 3Scroll down to Payment capture method
  4. 4Select “Manually capture payment for orders”
  5. 5Click Save
⚠️

7-day limit: Shopify Payments authorizations expire after 7 days. Always capture payment within this window after editing is complete.

⚡ Step 2 — Import the Account Editor Delayed Master Flow
✅ Step-by-Step Configuration

The Account Editor Delayed Master Flow is a pre-built Shopify Flow automation that holds payment capture for a configurable grace period, then captures automatically — triggering James & James to download the finalized order.

  1. 1Download the Account Editor Delayed Master Flow file (provided with this guide)
  2. 2In Shopify Admin, go to Apps → Shopify Flow
  3. 3Click Import and select the downloaded .flow file
  4. 4Configure the grace period timing to match your editing deadline (e.g., 30 minutes)
  5. 5Click Activate to enable the Flow
  6. 6Click Save
📥 Download Account Editor Delayed Master Flow

Download the pre-built Flow file — then import it into Shopify Flow

📝

How the Flow works: When a new order is created, the Flow waits for the configured grace period. After the wait, it automatically captures payment — changing the status from Authorized to Paid. James & James then downloads the order with all edits included.

📧 Step 3 — Contact James & James Support
🛑 Critical — Required for Proper Integration

James & James must update your account settings to handle removed product lines correctly. Without this configuration, removed items may still appear on pick lists with zero quantity.

🛑

Do not skip this step. If James & James does not update this setting, removed items will appear on pick lists with quantity = 0, causing confusion in the warehouse and potential fulfillment errors.

What to request: Email your James & James account manager or support team with the following:

Subject: Update Fulfillable Quantity Setting for Order Editing Integration

Hi James & James Team,

We’ve integrated Order Editing with our Shopify store to allow customers to modify orders post-purchase. To ensure this works correctly with your fulfillment system, we need to update our account settings.

Request: Please update our fulfillable quantity setting to not download removed product lines when syncing orders from Shopify.

Why this matters: Customers can remove items during the Order Editing window. When items are removed, the fulfillable quantity becomes zero. We need your system to recognize this and exclude those lines from the pick list.

Our setup:

  • Store: [Your Shopify store URL]
  • Account #: [Your James & James account number]
  • Integration: Delayed Payment Capture (orders download only after payment captured)

Expected behavior after this change:

  • Customer removes Item A from order
  • Item A fulfillable quantity = 0
  • Order syncs to James & James after editing window
  • Item A does not appear on pick list (because quantity = 0)

Please confirm this setting has been updated and let us know if you need any additional information.

📝

Wait for confirmation from James & James before going live. Do not start processing real orders until they confirm the fulfillable quantity setting has been updated on your account.

⏱️ Step 4 — Configure the Editing Deadline
✅ Step-by-Step Configuration

Set how long customers have to edit their order before the grace period expires and payment is captured. This deadline should match the timing configured in the Shopify Flow.

  1. 1Open Account Editor app settings
  2. 2Find the Editing Deadline setting
  3. 3Set the deadline to match your Flow timing (e.g., 30 minutes)
  4. 4Click Save
📝

Keep timings in sync: The editing deadline shown to the customer should match (or be slightly shorter than) the grace period in Shopify Flow. For example, if the Flow waits 30 minutes before capturing, set the editing deadline to 25–30 minutes so customers see an accurate countdown.

✏️ Step 5 — Your Account Editor Workflow
✅ How Every Order Works After Setup

Once setup is complete, this is the automated flow for every order:

  1. 1New order arrives → Payment = Authorized (James & James does not see it ✅)
  2. 2Grace period active → Customer can edit in Account Editor — add/remove items, change quantities, update address
  3. 3Grace period expires → Shopify Flow automatically captures payment
  4. 4Payment becomes Paid → James & James downloads the finalized order ✅
  5. 5Order is shipped with all edits included — no manual intervention needed
🔄 What Happens After This Setup
  • ✅Correct order total — removed items not counted
  • ✅Added items appear correctly in James & James
  • ✅Quantity changes reflected accurately
  • ✅Address changes included in the download
  • ✅No manual payment capture needed — fully automated
✅

Setup complete! James & James will always receive the final, correctly edited order. The entire flow is automated — no manual capture or James & James configuration required.

⚠️ Troubleshooting
Issue Likely Cause Fix
Orders appearing in James & James too early Payment capture not set to Manual, or Flow not activated Verify payment capture = “Manually capture payment for orders” and the Account Editor Delayed Master Flow is Active
Orders not appearing after grace period Flow is not capturing payment correctly Open Shopify Flow → check the run history for errors → re-import if needed
Edits not reflected in James & James Edits were made after the grace period ended Ensure all edits are completed before the grace period expires — James & James does not read live edits
Removed items still on pick list (quantity = 0) James & James fulfillable quantity setting not updated Contact James & James support — request they update the setting to exclude zero-quantity lines (see Step 3)
Shipping address not updated Address changed after grace period ended Ensure address changes are made during the editing window before payment is captured
Authorization expired (no payment captured) Grace period exceeds 7-day limit Reduce grace period to well under 7 days (recommended: 30 min – 2 hours)
🧠 Best Practices
  • Keep the grace period short — 30 minutes to 2 hours is ideal for most stores
  • Wait for James & James confirmation before going live — the fulfillable quantity setting must be updated on your account
  • Sync the editing deadline in Account Editor with the Flow timing so customers see an accurate countdown
  • Monitor Shopify Flow run history after launch to confirm payment captures are firing correctly
  • Test the full flow end-to-end before going live with real orders
